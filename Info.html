About the composer: Johann Philipp Kirnberger

GND, Wikidata

Johann Philipp Kirnberger (also Kernberg; 24 April 1721, Saalfeld – 27 July 1783, Berlin) was a german musician, composer (primarily of fugues), and music theorist.

Johann Philipp Kirnberger received violin and piano lessons in early childhood. After attending the Latin school in Coburg, he went to Johann Peter Kellner in Gräfenroda to further his musical knowledge. A later teacher and patron was the court organist Heinrich Nikolaus Gerber in Sondershausen.
A short stay of Kirnberger in Leipzig is documented for the year 1741. A possible apprenticeship with Johann Sebastian Bach is disputed and cannot be proven. After a brief stay in Dresden, he went to Poland for a longer period (1741 to 1751), where he held various positions, including harpsichordist and kapellmeister.
In the mid-1750s Kirnberger returned to Germany to take lessons in violin playing. After a position as violinist in the royal chapel in Potsdam, he later joined the chapel of Margrave Heinrich in Rheinsberg. He then held the position of composition teacher to Anna Amalie of Prussia until his death in 1758, and also served as Kapellmeister and musical advisor to the Prussian court. The majority of his compositions were written during this time.
His chamber music primarily consists of flute sonatas and trio sonatas; a flute sonata in E-flat minor is probably Kirnberger's most important work. He also constructed a total of three tempered tunings. The most balanced, third tuning is often referred to as the Kirnberger tuning.
Through Kirnberger's collecting activities, a whole series of Johann Sebastian Bach's works has come down to us - the most well-known is the so-called Kirnberger collection with the chorale arrangements BWV 690-713.

About the music: Der allezeit fertige Polonoisen und Menuettencomponist

Published: 1757 by George Ludewig Winter (Pub.)


Extract:

"Die Noten, welche auf den nachfolgenden wenigern Blättern erscheinen, sind der Stoff zu einer unzählbaren Menge von Polonoisen, Menuetten und dazu gehörigen Trios. Ein jeder der nur Würfel und Zahlen kennt, und Noten abschreiben kann, ist fähig, sich daraus so viele der genannten kleinen Stücke, vermittelst eines oder zweer Würfel zu componieren, als er nur verlanget.
Hat man mit einem Würfel eine Zahl geworfen; so suchet man in den Tabellen auf welchen die Nummern stehen, nachdem man nämlich Polonoisen, Menuetten oder Trios verfertigen will, in dem ersten Fache von der Linken zur Rechten die Zahl auf welchen man geworfen hat."
"Auf diese Art wird bei jedem Wurfe ein Tact, und mit sechs oder acht Würfen der erste Theil einer Polonoise, Menuet oder Trios fertig.
[...]
Will man sich der Würfel gar nicht bedienen; so darf man nur sich freywillig Zahlen annehmen, welche man will, die man geworfen haben könnte; und damit eben so verfahren, als wenn man gewürfel hätte."

Translation:
"The notes, which appear on the following few sheets, are the material for an innumerable number of polonoises, minuets and trios belonging to them. Anyone who knows only dice and numbers, and can copy notes, is able to compose as many of the aforementioned small pieces from them as he requires, by means of one or two dice.
When one has thrown a number with a dice, one looks in the tables on which the numbers are written, after one wants to compose polonoises, minuets or trios, in the first compartment from the left to the right, for the number on which one has thrown."
"In this way, with each throw, one bar is completed, and with six or eight throws, the first part of a polonoise, minuet or trio is finished.
[...]
If one does not want to make use of the dice at all, then one may only freely assume numbers that one wants, that one could have thrown; and proceed with this just as if one had thrown the dice."

About target audience:

Kirnberger wanted to target and reach music lovers (Liebhaber der Musik) with his book rather than professional composers, who would still be able to take something away from the collection.

"Einen wirklichen Compositionsverständigen wird zum wenigsten, die in diesen Tabellen vorkommende, so mannigfaltige Ausbildung eben derselben Harmonie, über einerley Grundstimme, nicht ganz zuwider seyn. Ein angehender Tonsetzer kann vielleicht darauf einigen Vortheil, zur Sammlung eines Vorraths von Veränderungen der musikalischen Figuren, ziehen."

Translation:
"A real composition expert will, at least, not be completely repelled by the manifold formation of the same harmony over the same basic voice that occurs in these tables. An aspiring composer can perhaps derive some benefit from them, for the collection of a stock of changes in musical figures."
