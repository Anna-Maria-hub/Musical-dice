What is a musical dice game?

Musical dice games (“musikalische Würfelspiele”) were very popular in Western Europe, especially in the 18th century, and are not limited only to throwing dice per se, but also involve the random selection of numbers. The earliest printed result of this compositional practice is "Der allezeit fertige Polonoisen- und Menuettencomponist" (The all-time ready polonaise and minuet composer) by Johann Philipp Kirnberger. First published in 1757, Kirnberger's inclusion of the performers in the seemingly randomly diced compositional process was probably an unconscious pioneer of the later concepts of aleatorics.

These seemingly random pieces are actually composed in a carefully thought-out way and are based on simple harmonic sequences that are performed slightly differently in the variations for each bar. The practices themselves consist of multiple short measures of music, tables full of numbers used to select specific bars given by either dice rolls or choosing a number and rules on how to use both to achieve a full piece in its characteristic style.
